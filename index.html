We call it Tatting

Tatting may have developed from netting and decorative ropework as sailors and fishermen would put together motifs for girlfriends and wives at home.

Decorative ropework employed on ships includes techniques (esp. coxcombing) that show striking similarity with tatting.

The history of the Tatting

Some believe tatting originated over 200 years ago, often citing shuttles seen in 18th-century paintings of women such as Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz,
Princess Marie Adelaide of France, and Anne, Countess of Albemarle. A close inspection of those paintings, however, shows that the shuttles in question are too large to be tatting shuttles,
and that they are actually knotting shuttles.

There is no documentation of or examples of tatted lace that dates prior to 1800. All available evidence shows that tatting originated in the early 19th century.
